One system,
day and after dark.

REALITY's silkscreen DNA — cream paper, ink rectangles, hard down-shadows, misregistered color — carried into a reconciled Night theme and stretched into a poster language built for the nightlife: parties, DJ sets, live music and the bar.

How to use this page

One tokened theme, day and night. Surfaces flip, borders & text ride --fg, and shadows invert to cream. The palette is a tight 3 majors + 4 minors on Montserrat (uppercase, wide-tracked) + Space Grotesk body, with the Montserrat Alternates wordmark, hard corners, and the flat riso down-shadow.

The palette is category-coding. Pink for parties, blue for music, yellow for drink deals. Use the controls up top to switch Day / Night, flip green & purple between the specced and lifted values, and swap the category accent — the whole page and every poster re-skins live.

Roles — a fixed job for each colour
Red · Action

Imperatives and primary actions — join, confirm, “do it now”. The loudest call on a screen; aim for one per view.

Blue · Information

Informational and wayfinding — links, info states, secondary emphasis. Present, not urgent.

Yellow · Notice

Highlights, notices and lower-priority button fills. A fill behind ink only — never set as text or thin marks on cream.

Minors · Accents

Pink, amber, green, purple — sparingly, for category dots, misregister offsets and small flourishes. Not for body text or large fills.

Ink + Cream · Structure

The frame everything sits on — text, borders, surfaces, and neutral controls like the Day/Night toggle. Colour rides on top; it never replaces the frame.

07

DNA

Four rules carry the whole look — unchanged in the dark.
01
Hard corners

Zero border-radius — cards, buttons, posters are sharp rectangles. Rounding reads as off-brand.

02
Flat down-shadow

Big vertical offset, almost no blur, no spread. A riso shift, not a soft ambient glow.

03
Hover = lift

Interactive things rise a few px and the shadow deepens; they press back down on click.

04
Misregister

Offset accent duplicates behind a hero block — a print that didn't quite register. The night's energy.
