The expansion · photographic ink

Print the photo,
don't place it.

REALITY already prints like a riso — misregistered echoes, flat down-shadows, a locked three-ink palette. Photos should obey the same press. Instead of dropping a full-colour image into a silkscreen poster, every photo runs through an ink treatment so it belongs to the system. Six directions below — each leans on the riso angle and mixes it with one more idea. Swap the ink, flip the paper, drop your own shot.

01

Six treatments

All six read the same source photo and the same ink. Live — every control above repaints them.
01
Riso × Duotone

Two-Colour Sep

The bread-and-butter riso move. Luminance is remapped onto two inks — paper for the lights, the accent for the shadows. Full tonal photo, zero off-palette colour.

Map · shadow → ink, highlight → paper · the safest, most legible option
02
Riso × Misregister

Off-Register

Your signature, applied to photos. The image prints twice — accent and its partner ink — shifted a few millimetres so colour fringes bleed at every edge. The print that didn't quite line up.

Two passes · offset + overprint · the most on-brand of the set
03
Riso × Newsprint

Halftone Screen

One ink, rendered as a rotated dot screen — dots fatten in the shadows, thin to nothing in the light. Cheap-print energy, gig-poster grit. Best on high-contrast shots.

Single ink · dot size = darkness · tune with the dot-screen slider
04
Riso × Silkscreen

Banded Tones

Tones crushed into a few flat steps, each step snapped to a palette ink. No gradients — pure silkscreen flatness, the way a hand-pulled print separates. Posterised, not photographic.

Quantise · ink → accent → paper ramp · set the step count with Bands
05
Riso × Flat Field

Ink Cutout

The lit subject knocks out of a solid accent field and prints in a single ink. Maximum poster punch — reads from across a dark room. Pairs with the accent-field type-slam layout.

Threshold · subject in ink, field in accent · loud and graphic
06
Riso × Wet Overlap

Overprint

Two flat ink fields lock together and a third colour blooms where they overlap — like wet inks bleeding on press. Pink over blue makes purple for free. The richest, most unexpected look.

Two flat fields · multiply / screen overlap · breeds a bonus hue

04

Why lean in

01
One press, everything

Type, shapes and now photos all separate into the same inks. A poster reads as a single printed object, not artwork with a photo pasted on.

02
Palette stays locked

Treatments can only deposit system inks, so a phone snapshot can't drag an off-brand colour into the poster. The palette polices itself.

03
Cheap shots look intentional

Blurry, badly-lit nightlife photos are the reality. Halftone and cutout turn that grit into the point instead of hiding it.
