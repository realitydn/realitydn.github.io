The feature menu, compressed

Three layers,
one journey.

All those features collapse into a small grammar. Every REALITY poster is the same three decisions: which Frame it ships in, which Parts are on it, and where the Dials sit. And the dials all move together along one road — Community → Nightlife. Slide everything left and you get a calm daytime talk; slide right and the same skeleton turns into a wild night. This is the model the generator runs on.

The Dials

Seven spectrums, all pointing the same way: calm community on the left, wild nightlife on the right.

Palette

Day→Night

The master switch every other dial follows. Day is ink on cream — legible, warm, daylight. Night is cream on ink — the room goes dark and the type glows. Community events live in day; nightlife lives after dark. Pick this first and the rest of the dials have a home to move within.

Cool→Hot

D3

Tilt

Square→Skewed

How far the boxes break off the grid's right angles. Zero tilt is orthogonal and professional — everything squared to the module. Crank it and boxes rotate, overlap and misregister like a rushed silkscreen pull. Tilt is the single biggest tell between a composed community bill and a kinetic night — use it sparingly or it stops reading as REALITY.

D4

Symmetry

Centered→Offset

Where the cluster balances. Centered mirrors everything on the spine — orderly, welcoming, easy to read at a glance. Offset slams the title to one edge and lets the info hang off the other, creating tension and motion. Centered says "come in"; offset says "something's happening."

D5

Density

Airy→Packed

How many Parts ride the grid. Airy is the nine essentials with room to breathe — one idea, clearly stated. Packed stacks the optional blocks: lineup, specials, tiers, stamps. Community events are usually one thing (a talk, a quiz); nightlife sells many at once (who's on, what's cheap, when's free) — so density climbs as you move right.

Type Weight

Quiet→Slam

How loud the title speaks. Thin display (100) is elegant and measured — it trusts the reader to lean in. Heavy slam (800) is physical and unmissable — it shouts across a dark room. The weight is the title's tone of voice, and it climbs with the energy of the night.

D7

Surface

Solid→Sheer

How much photo shows through the boxes. Solid is opaque ink or cream — maximum contrast, the photo a frame around the words. Sheer opens the box to outline or a frosted scrim so the image breathes through. Solid when the words must win; sheer when the photo is the star. Every state keeps the down-shadow.

03

Color, With Reasons

The accent dial, unpacked. Each color carries a register — pick by what the night should feel like, not by taste.

Cool side

— community, daylight, calm
Major
Blue #18A7E0

Open sky, trust, daytime. The default community calm — talks, coffee mornings, markets. The safest accent; reads as welcoming to anyone.

Minor
Green #43B02A

Fresh, growing, wholesome. Wellness, makers, anything outdoors or food-led. Warmer and more grounded than blue without losing the calm.

Major
Yellow #FDDF00

Sunshine, optimism, volume. High-energy daytime — fairs, family days, anything loud but still wholesome. The bridge toward the warm side.

Hot side

— nightlife, after dark, energy
Minor
Amber #FDB515

Golden hour, neon warmth. The bridge between day and night — sundowners, early-evening sets, happy hour. Energy without full heat.

Minor
Purple #6E3179

Mystery, the small hours, glow. Deep house, after-parties, anything that lives past midnight. Lifts to a brighter glow on the night palette.

Minor
Pink #ED1B72

Heat, flirtation, pop. The party default — dancefloors, late DJ sets, anything fun and physical. The signature nightlife lead.

Major
Red #ED2224

Urgency, intensity, alarm. The biggest nights, headliners, sell-outs, last call. Use it when the message is "don't miss this."

Why this matters for the build

If the generator stores a poster as seven dial values + a Parts list + a Frame, then "make a nightlife version" is literally dragging the dials right — no re-layout. Presets become saved coordinates (Community, Sundowner, Nightlife); a recurring series locks its own coordinate so every week matches. That's the elegant core to build toward — the feature menu is just what each dial and part can do.
