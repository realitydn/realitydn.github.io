The armature beneath the imagery

One grid,
every kind of night.

A single structural skeleton for every REALITY poster — regular enough to lay out fast, dynamic enough to tell a talky community afternoon apart from a wild night on the same visual language. You bring the full-bleed photo; the system places the title, the details, and the ticket on a grid that survives the crop from Instagram to WhatsApp.

How to read this spec

The frame is fixed; the energy flexes. Every poster is a 5:7 canvas on a 108px square module, with the title and details locked into a centre safe square so they survive WhatsApp's crop. What moves between event types is the palette (day vs night), the symmetry, the accent loudness, and how many info boxes ride the grid.

Static examples first, generator later. These are reference layouts to react to. Once the structure feels right, it becomes the rule-set for the poster generator — you'll pick an event type, drop a photo, type the details, and it assembles on this grid.

One honest tradeoff up top: the bottom ticket band (wordmark · address · website · QR) sits below the WhatsApp safe square on purpose — the square chat thumbnail shows the punchy title cluster; the full poster and IG portrait show the ticket. Where the QR must survive the square crop, use the corner-ticket variant in §06.

Why 5:7 on a square module

The tall 5:7 frame privileges the Instagram grid; the square module keeps everything snapping cleanly. The crops nest exactly on the grid — so the safe zone isn't a guess, it's two rows in from top and bottom.

  • 5:7
    Full canvas — 1080 × 1512

    The poster and the IG portrait post. Photo bleeds to all four edges; only atmosphere lives in the outer rows.

  • 4:5
    IG feed crop

    Instagram trims to 4:5 in-feed — ≈0.75 module off top & bottom. Keep nothing vital in that sliver.

  • 1:1
    Safe square — the WhatsApp thumb

    Centred 1080×1080, inset exactly 2 modules (216px) top & bottom. Title + all details live here. This is the cluster-in-the-middle rule, drawn to the grid.

The nine, grouped

  • M
    Marquee — title + tagline

    The slam. Biggest type on the grid, top of the safe square. Tagline rides directly beneath in Space Grotesk.

  • C
    Credits — host · day · time

    Who's running it and when. Day + time often double as the when kicker above the title; the host gets its own box. Nightlife stacks extras here — DJ lineup, drink specials.

  • Ticket — wordmark · address · website · QR

    The unchanging anchor. Same four marks on every poster so the brand and the scan are never in doubt. Band by day, corner cluster after dark.

Each box keeps the riso down-shadow — a hard straight-down shift — so the structure reads as stacked paper even over a busy photo.

03

Community ↔ Nightlife

Same skeleton, four levers. Palette does the heaviest lifting: day for the talk, night for the wild.

Community · symmetric, by day

Day palette (ink on cream). Everything centred on the spine, calm accent — blue or green. Air between boxes. Reads as welcoming and legible.

Nightlife · asymmetric, after dark

Night palette (cream on ink). Title slams left and offsets, loud accent — pink or red — with a misregistered print. More boxes (DJs, specials) and a corner ticket. Reads loud and kinetic.

04

Grid Options

Three ways the same square module can carry a layout — from straight-laced to broken.

A · Symmetric stack

Everything mirrored on the centre column. The professional default — what you've been building. Best for community and any info-light bill.

B · Broad / narrow split

A 6 / 4 column break — headline owns the wide left, a tall accent box (lineup, specials, price) owns the narrow right. Asymmetry without chaos.

C · Offset stagger

Boxes break the grid — slight rotation, overlap, a corner ticket. Misregister as layout. Reserved for the wildest nights; use sparingly or it stops reading as REALITY.

06

Open Questions

Three variables to settle before the generator. Every option keeps the riso down-shadow.

A · How the ticket sits

Band

Full-width base rule. Maximum presence, but falls below the WhatsApp square.

Corner cluster

Compressed to one corner. Frees the frame for asymmetric nights; reads as a stamp.

Pulled into centre

QR + wordmark inside the safe square. Survives every crop — at the cost of the clean base edge.
